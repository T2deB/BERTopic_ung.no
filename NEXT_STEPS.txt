Parameter tuning playbook
-------------------------
Goal: cleaner clusters (higher purity / higher mean_primary_membership)
- Increase FUZZY_N_CLUSTERS (e.g. 50 -> 60)
- Increase FUZZY_M toward 1 (harder assignments; 1.0 = hard clustering)
- Raise ASSIGN_MIN_MARGIN to push ambiguous docs to unassigned

Goal: more coverage (fewer unassigned / lower boundary rate)
- Lower ASSIGN_MIN_MARGIN to 0.02 or 0.015
- Lower FUZZY_M toward 2+ (softer memberships; 2.0 is default)
- Optionally lower ASSIGN_MIN_SIM to 0.42

Important: change one thing at a time.

How to interpret cluster_sim and cluster_margin
-----------------------------------------------
Definitions (from assign_all in bottom_up_clustering.py):
- cluster_sim = cosine similarity to the winning centroid (best_sim)
- cluster_margin = best_sim - second_best_sim

Intuition:
- High cluster_sim + high cluster_margin
  -> confident assignment (close to one cluster and clearly farther from alternatives)
- High cluster_sim + low cluster_margin
  -> near a boundary between two plausible clusters (theme overlap)
- Low cluster_sim + high cluster_margin
  -> not very close to any centroid, but still "least bad" toward one cluster
- Low cluster_sim + low cluster_margin
  -> weak/ambiguous point; good candidate for unassigned bucket

Operationally in this project:
- A row is forced to unassigned (cluster_id = -1) when either
  cluster_sim < ASSIGN_MIN_SIM OR cluster_margin < ASSIGN_MIN_MARGIN.

How to interpret fuzzy outputs
-------------------------------
- mean_primary_membership in cluster_overview.csv
  -> Average confidence that documents assigned to this cluster truly belong here.
  -> Values below 0.5 suggest the cluster is thematically diffuse.

- boundary_doc_count / boundary_pct
  -> Documents where the top membership score < 0.5.
  -> High boundary rate in a cluster = overlapping themes; consider splitting.

- rank_diff in topic_words_fuzzy.csv
  -> TIadj_rank minus freq_rank
  -> Positive: term ranks higher in fuzzy scoring than raw frequency
     (term is characteristic for high-membership docs)
  -> Negative: term is frequent but less characteristic of the cluster core

- overlap_count in topic_word_comparison.csv
  -> Number of shared terms between fuzzy top-5 and frequency top-5 for a cluster
  -> Low overlap = fuzzy weighting surfaces different terms than raw frequency
