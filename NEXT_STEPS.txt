Parameter tuning playbook
-------------------------
Goal: cleaner clusters (higher purity)
- Keep ASSIGN_MIN_SIM at 0.44 or increase to 0.46
- Keep ASSIGN_MIN_MARGIN at 0.025 or increase to 0.03
- Expect more unassigned rows as purity rises

Goal: more coverage (fewer unassigned)
- Lower ASSIGN_MIN_MARGIN to 0.02 (or 0.015)
- Optionally lower ASSIGN_MIN_SIM to 0.42
- Expect some clusters to become less pure

Important: change one thing at a time.

How to interpret cluster_sim and cluster_margin
-----------------------------------------------
Definitions (from assign_all in bottom_up_clustering.py):
- cluster_sim = cosine similarity to the winning centroid (best_sim)
- cluster_margin = best_sim - second_best_sim

Intuition:
- High cluster_sim + high cluster_margin
  -> confident assignment (close to one cluster and clearly farther from alternatives)
- High cluster_sim + low cluster_margin
  -> near a boundary between two plausible clusters (theme overlap)
- Low cluster_sim + high cluster_margin
  -> not very close to any centroid, but still "least bad" toward one cluster
- Low cluster_sim + low cluster_margin
  -> weak/ambiguous point; good candidate for unassigned bucket

Operationally in this project:
- A row is forced to unassigned (cluster_id = -1) when either
  cluster_sim < ASSIGN_MIN_SIM OR cluster_margin < ASSIGN_MIN_MARGIN.
- This means margin acts as a "separability" gate and sim acts as an
  "absolute fit" gate.

Practical guidance for large mixed clusters (example: cluster 150)
------------------------------------------------------------------
When a large cluster contains many different question types, increase assignment
strictness first (before changing UMAP/HDBSCAN), then review what moves to
unassigned.

Recommended sequence:
1) Raise margin only:
   - ASSIGN_MIN_MARGIN: 0.025 -> 0.03 (or 0.035)
   - Keep ASSIGN_MIN_SIM unchanged.
2) Re-run and inspect:
   - cluster size drop for the mixed cluster
   - thematic coherence in sample_for_labeling.csv
   - increase in A_unassigned_questions.csv
3) If still mixed, raise similarity modestly:
   - ASSIGN_MIN_SIM: 0.44 -> 0.46
4) Stop when extra strictness mostly removes useful in-cluster examples.

Rule of thumb with your overview table:
- sim_mean around 0.82-0.86 is common, so assignment quality is often decided by
  margin for borderline items.
- clusters with high margin_mean are usually easier to label cleanly.
- if margin_mean is low and top examples are semantically broad, tighten margin first.
